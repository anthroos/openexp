# Dog-Food Test — 2026-04-26

First end-to-end self-test of the v2 publishing pipeline. The team produced an experience pack from a real closed deal and tested whether retrieval surfaces it correctly.

## What was tested

- Ingested the seed experience (`d49e0997`) into the running Qdrant collection via `add_memory`.
- Ran three queries that the seed should match.
- Re-ran the failing query after introducing **per-step indexing**.

## Round 1 — summary-only indexing

The seed was added as a single memory carrying the experience's `searchable_summary` as content.

| Query | Did seed surface in top-5? | Top result |
|-------|----------------------------|------------|
| "free pilot batch acquisition contract signed" | **Yes — #1** (hybrid 0.61) | seed itself |
| "client went silent for three weeks after pilot how to re-engage" | **No** | older Experience Library labels (PR #34) (silent 5d, not 3w — different pattern) |
| "inbound discovery call counterparty NDA service agreement" | **Yes — #2** (hybrid 0.60) | another inbound experience at #1 |

**Finding 1:** Summary-only indexing succeeds for queries that match the summary directly, fails for queries about *patterns inside the trajectory*. The 26 steps each carry a distinct moment (silence period, re-engagement, PM introduction, etc.) — none of those moments is searchable when only the wrapper is indexed.

## Round 2 — per-step indexing

Generated `experiences/d49e0997/steps.indexable.jsonl` (one JSON record per trajectory step). Added the four critical pattern-bearing steps to Qdrant:

- step 11 (day +25) — silence period begins
- step 12 (day +49) — counterparty re-engages
- step 13 (day +50) — sync call confirms moving to commercial
- step 22 (day +57) — founder signs from own side late evening

Re-ran the failing query.

### Round 2.1 — metadata-heavy step format

Format used: `EXPERIENCE STEP — d49e0997 step 11, day +25, observation/silence\n\nSource: ...\n\n<content>`

**Result:** seed steps still **did not surface in top-5**. older hand-crafted Experience Library labels (`EXPERIENCE: ...lesson... APPLIES WHEN: ...`) dominated by both BM25 and semantic. The metadata-heavy lead diluted the actual content's signal in the embedding.

### Round 2.2 — structural-fact-led format

Same step content, reformatted: structural fact at the top, source metadata at the bottom.

> Counterparty silent for ~3 weeks after free pilot batch delivery. They had implicitly promised a review around the day-24 mark, but ~3 weeks pass with no substantive reply on commercial terms. The silence breaks only when the counterparty re-engages with a call proposal, without acknowledging the gap.
>
> Source: experience d49e0997 (acquisition, closed_won grade 1.0), step 11 of 24, observed at day +25 of the trajectory.

**Result:** seed step jumped to **#3** in top-5 for the same failing query. Raw vector score 0.71. older pre-labeled experiences still take #1 and #2.

## Findings

1. **Per-step indexing is necessary, not optional.** Summary-only retrieval misses pattern queries entirely. With per-step indexing in the right format, seed steps reach top-5.
2. **Format matters as much as granularity.** Structural-fact-led content beat metadata-led content with the same source by ~0.12 in vector score. Embedding lead bytes carry disproportionate weight.
3. **Pre-labeled experiences (PR #34 Experience Library) still beat us on retrieval today.** They have hand-crafted titles and `EXPERIENCE: <punchy lesson>` openers — both BM25 and semantic optimize for that. We rejected pre-labeling at step level on methodological grounds (would inject labeler bias into the eventual training corpus). This is a deliberate trade-off, not a bug to fix.
4. **The trade-off has a deadline.** Until a model is trained on the corpus and can rank by outcome rather than keyword, retrieval today is weaker than the older Experience Library labels. That is the cost of clean training data. Two paths forward: (a) accept weak retrieval until we have ≥30 graded trajectories and a baseline scorer, or (b) reintroduce **structural** (not interpretive) labels at step level — e.g., a one-line "what happened" header per step that summarizes the structural fact. Path (b) does not violate "no pre-labeling" because it does not predict signal quality; it just compresses content for retrieval. Decision deferred to a follow-up PR.

## Implications for the prompts

`prompts/extract_experience.md` should be updated to require:

- A `steps.indexable.jsonl` artifact alongside the trajectory and experience YAML.
- Each step record content formatted **structural-fact-led**: the substantive observation in the first 1–2 sentences, followed by source metadata (`Source: experience <id>, step <N> of <total>, day +<D>`).
- Optionally (TBD): a `step_lead` field — a single sentence summarizing the step's structural fact — for retrieval without violating no-pre-labeling.

## Author note

This dog-food test was the first time the v2 pipeline was applied to a real artifact in a live retrieval setup. It surfaced both confirmations (publishing pipeline works end-to-end) and a real feature gap (per-step indexing required, format matters). Honest failure recording is more useful here than a clean success story; the file is preserved as the trajectory of v2's first self-test.
